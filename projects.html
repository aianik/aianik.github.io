01

Does Transparency About Training Data Change How Users Trust AI?

Two studies, 44 participants. When users could see what an AI was trained on, trust tracked data quality directly: revealing problems lowered trust, revealing balanced data raised it. The effect held regardless of ML expertise.

02

Does Explanation Presentation Format Affect How Users Assess and Understand AI?

Same information, two formats, different outcomes. Narrative with visuals produced 66% high-accuracy critiques. Structured Q&A produced 42%. Format alone, not content, determined how well users could evaluate the AI.

03

How Much Detail Should a Training Data Explanation Include?

Conventional wisdom says simplify AI explanations. This study pushed back: users produced more comments and more accurate ones with detailed explanations, accepted the extra cognitive load willingly, and found progressive disclosure improved learning, not efficiency.

04

How Do We Measure AI Literacy: Developing a Scale That Captures Both Knowledge and Confidence?

476 participants, two independent validation samples. The scale works, but the real finding is the calibration pattern: people who think they know AI the best are the most likely to overestimate what they actually know.
